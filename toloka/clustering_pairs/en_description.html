Description

In this task, you have to decide whether a pair of news corresponds to the same thread.

Threads are sets of articles about the same event or topic, revealing very similar information.

Generally, it should be enough for the reader to read one article from a thread to learn everything (or nearly everything) from that thread. But to learn everything about the topic – they’ll need to read several threads.

Criteria

Two news correspond to the same thread when:

  • They happened at the same time.
  • They provide the same data, like a stock price or a number of casualties.
  • They occurred in the same place.
  • Their titles are interchangeable: you can switch them, and no one will see the difference.

Two news do NOT correspond to the same thread when:

  • They contain different facts.
  • One news describes some event (or preparation to it), while other reports a result of the event.
  • One news describes an event, while other reports a comment of any involved person on the event.

You are given two article titles, their first few lines and a link to the original articles. You can find out the publication date or some number from those original articles. Using this information, you should be able to determine correspondence of the news to a single thread.

Examples

1. Articles “The World Health Organization declared a global emergency over the new coronavirus.” and “Coronavirus declared global health emergency by WHO” correspond to the same thread.

While news “WHO declares coronavirus outbreak a global health emergency” and “Nations should avoid overreacting on coronavirus, says China as WHO declares global emergency.” correspond to different threads.

2. News “The UK has confirmed four cases of the virus.” and “Coronavirus: Four cases confirmed in UK.” correspond to the same thread.

While articles “The UK has confirmed four cases of the virus.” and “Coronavirus: number of confirmed UK cases rises to eight.” correspond to different threads.

3. Articles “Japan may call off Tokyo Olympics over coronavirus fears.” and “Tokyo Olympic organisers emphasise the Games will go ahead despite coronavirus.” correspond to different threads, because the information differes.

4. News “NASA astronauts Christina Koch and Jessica Meir prepare for a spacewalk” and “NASA Astronauts Complete the First All-Female Spacewalk” correspond to different threads, because the second news describes the result of the event announced in the first one.
